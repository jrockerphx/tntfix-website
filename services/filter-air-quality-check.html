Filter & Air Quality Check

Ensure proper filtration, identify air quality issues, and get recommendations for healthier indoor air.

What This Service Includes

Proper filtration is essential for HVAC efficiency and indoor air quality. We inspect your current setup, identify issues, and provide tailored recommendations for your home.

  • Inspect current filter size, type, and condition
  • Verify proper filter fit (no air bypass)
  • Check for return air leaks
  • Measure airflow and static pressure
  • Assess ductwork visible sections for leaks/damage
  • Identify signs of indoor air quality problems
  • Recommend appropriate filter MERV rating
  • Provide filter replacement schedule guidance
  • Discuss air quality improvement options
  • Install new filter (standard size included)

Why Air Filtration Matters

Your HVAC filter does more than protect equipmentâ€”it's your first line of defense against dust, allergens, and pollutants. In Phoenix's dusty environment, proper filtration is especially important.

  • Health: removes dust, pollen, pet dander, and other allergens
  • Equipment protection: prevents dust buildup on coils and components
  • Energy efficiency: proper airflow reduces energy consumption
  • System longevity: clean systems last years longer

Common Filter Problems We Find

  • Wrong size filter: gaps allow unfiltered air to bypass
  • Installed backwards: reduces effectiveness and damages filter
  • Too restrictive: high-MERV filters can reduce airflow on older systems
  • Neglected replacement: clogged filters restrict airflow and strain system
  • Poor quality filters: cheap fiberglass filters provide minimal protection
  • Missing filter: some homes have filter slots but no filters installed!

Filter Recommendations

We'll recommend the right filter type based on your system capabilities, budget, and air quality goals:

  • MERV 8: basic filtration, good for newer systems with good airflow
  • MERV 11: better allergen capture, suitable for most homes
  • MERV 13: high efficiency, excellent for allergies/asthma (verify system compatibility first)
  • Pleated vs. fiberglass: pleated filters offer significantly better filtration
  • Washable filters: pros/cons for your specific situation

Replacement Schedule

Phoenix homes typically need filter changes every 30-60 days due to heavy dust and extended cooling season. Homes with pets, smokers, or construction nearby may need monthly changes. We'll provide a personalized schedule based on your situation.

Air Quality Improvement Options

Beyond basic filtration, we can discuss and install:

  • UV lights: kill mold, bacteria, and viruses in the air handler
  • Whole-home air purifiers: advanced filtration systems
  • Humidifiers: combat Phoenix's dry air (winter months)
  • Duct sealing: prevent unfiltered air from entering system
  • ERV/HRV systems: bring fresh outdoor air in while maintaining efficiency
