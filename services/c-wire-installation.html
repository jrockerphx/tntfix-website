What’s a C-Wire (and why it matters)

Most smart thermostats require a C-wire (common wire) to provide constant 24V power. If your old thermostat didn’t need one, this is the #1 reason smart thermostat installs fail or become unreliable.

Without a proper C-wire, thermostats often rely on batteries or “power stealing,” which can cause disconnects, HVAC odd behavior, and dead batteries.

Our Solutions

  • Option 1: C-Wire Adapter — $179 (about 1 hour). Install an adapter at the HVAC system. No wire fishing through walls. Works for most systems.
  • Option 2: Run a New C-Wire — $249–$399 (about 1.5–2 hours). Fish a new wire from HVAC to thermostat. Most reliable solution. Price depends on accessibility.
