Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common home repair questions

What's the difference between a handyman and a licensed contractor?

In Handymen cannot work on structural, major gas, or major electrical systems. Licensed contractors handle larger jobs and specialized work that requires permits. We're upfront about what falls within our scope—if your project needs a licensed contractor, we'll tell you.

Do I need to provide materials, or do you bring them?

It depends on the service. For most fixture and device installs, we ask you to purchase the item you want (faucet, ceiling fan, etc.) so you get exactly what you like. For basic supplies like drywall compound, screws, or wire nuts, we bring what's needed. Our service pages specify what's included. You save significantly when you buy what you need and have someone install it for you, the markup with many contractors is 100% or more than he cost of the item, this covers the potential cost of your installer warrantying the item if it fails in the first year. You get the same warranty from nearly all manufacturers without the markup when you purchase it yourself - be sure to keep your receipts and paperwork of course.

What are your "Honey-Do" packages?

Our Honey-Do Half Day ($449 for up to 4 hours) and Full Day ($749 for up to 8 hours) packages let you knock out a punch list of small tasks in one visit. You provide the list, we help prioritize, and we work through as much as time allows. Great before guests arrive or when your to-do list has gotten out of hand.
